Task 1 · Step 4

Target Segment

After applying the affordability and behavior filters, I further prioritize users based on spending power and planning behavior to identify the most attractive segment.

Planning behavior comparison
Independent planners
  • Can complete the trip on their own.
  • Use OTA mainly for search and comparison.
  • Lower platform dependence.
  • Lower conversion leverage.
Semi-planners
  • Have travel intent, but still need help completing the trip.
  • Rely on OTA for key trip components and execution.
  • Higher platform dependence.
  • Most responsive to friction reduction.
The difference is not travel intent, but platform dependency — semi-planners depend on the platform to complete the trip, while independent planners do not.
Spending power comparison
Affluent
  • High spending power and flexible budget.
  • Lower dependence on OTA support.
  • More likely to use concierge, agencies, or direct booking.
  • Market size is very small.
Middle-class
  • Large enough to scale and realistically able to travel.
  • More dependent on OTA to optimize cost and reduce complexity.
  • More responsive to pricing, bundling, and planning support.
  • Stronger conversion potential at scale.
The difference is not just ability to pay, but dependency — affluent users can travel without the platform, while middle-class users rely on the platform to make the trip work.

Task 1 · Step 5

Pain Points & Strategy

These are the key conversion blockers for middle-class semi-planners when traveling to China.

Pain Point 1
Language barrier and low execution confidence
  • Indonesia ranks 80/116 in English proficiency.
  • China relies heavily on Mandarin-based systems.
  • Independent navigation is difficult without guidance.
↗ EF EPI 2024
Trip.com strategy
  • Launch Bahasa Indonesia small-group tours at top China destinations (e.g., 1-day guided options).
  • Focus on key high-friction scenarios (first-time, family, complex cities).
Pain Point 2
Payment access and flexibility gap
  • Trip.com lacks key local payment access, especially GoPay.
  • Only ~5% credit card penetration in Indonesia.
  • China trips require large upfront payments (~Rp 15M).
↗ Statista
Trip.com strategy
  • First integrate GoPay to close the most important local wallet access gap.
  • Then add PayLater / BNPL options through GoPay PayLater, Kredivo, or Akulaku.
Competitive gap
Traveloka already offers broader local payment access and PayLater, while Trip.com still lacks both layers.

Pain Point 3
Infrastructure complexity and planning burden
  • Complex high-speed rail booking system.
  • Fragmented local transport and service platforms.
  • Users struggle to execute multi-city itineraries.
↗ China Railway 12306
Trip.com strategy
  • Turn rail + hotel + itinerary into structured trip templates.
  • Offer pre-built routes for common China journeys and family travel.
Pain Point 4
Unmet halal travel demand
  • 89% prioritize halal food availability.
  • 60% willing to pay premium for halal compliance.
  • Limited visibility of halal-friendly options.
↗ Vero x GMO-Z.com Research (2025)
Trip.com strategy
  • Add halal filters and visible trust signals across listings.
  • Offer curated local halal itineraries for major destinations or special tours for Muslim travelers.
These frictions prevent high-intent users from converting into bookings.

Task 2 · Step 2

Campaign Mechanics

These are not generic tactics. They are strategic levers designed specifically for middle-class semi-planners who need control, reassurance, and better payment confidence.

Mechanic 1
Modular Family Bundle Discount
Control + savings
What it is

Users choose hotels, rail, and tours with some freedom, while the platform automatically turns those choices into a bundled offer.

Why it works

It keeps enough control for semi-planners, adds a visible price advantage, and lowers perceived booking risk through flexible cancellation and disruption protection.

Strategic role: push users toward deeper bundled booking without making the experience feel like a rigid local package.
Mechanic 2
Muslim Trust Infrastructure
Community trust
What it is

Use Muslim family creators, halal-focused trip proof, and culturally relevant travel reassurance to make China feel safer and more socially validated.

Why it works

For Muslim family travelers, trust is not built only by the platform. It is borrowed from the community and then reinforced through platform support.

Strategic role: reduce hesitation at the consideration stage, where cultural fit and fear of mistakes can block conversion.

Task 2 · Step 4

Seasonality Strategy

Seasonality changes both user mindset and conversion readiness, so the campaign objective should shift with the calendar.

Ramadan
Plan / inspire
User mindset

Booking slows, but research and future-trip planning increase.

What to push

Inspiration, itinerary examples, and trust-building content rather than hard conversion messaging.

Eid / Lebaran
Convert demand
User mindset

Accumulated family demand converts quickly once travel windows become concrete.

What to push

Modular family bundles, urgency, and clear booking simplicity for family-led trips.

Off-peak
Stimulate demand
User mindset

Intent is weaker and users need more financial or flexibility reassurance to commit.

What to push

Price framing, flexible cancellation, and BNPL as demand stimulants.
